For Realtors · 90 seconds

The Voice Extractor

One prompt. Five of your past listings. Your personal Voice Manifest, saved as a Skill.

What this does: Claude reads 5 listings you wrote yourself, then extracts the patterns that make your writing yours — sentence length, the phrases you actually use, the words you never use, your closing pattern. The output is a Voice Manifest you save as a Claude Skill.

Why it works: default Claude writes in the average realtor's voice. After this Skill is saved, Claude reads your Voice Manifest at the start of every listing task and writes the way you write.

What you need: 5 listings you wrote before AI (or your best ones since), and 90 seconds.

How to use it

1. Open Claude. Start a new conversation.
2. Paste the prompt below. Replace the bracketed sections with your 5 listings.
3. Claude returns your Voice Manifest. Copy it.
4. Save it as a Skill: create a file voice.md, paste the Manifest, save under your .claude/skills/ folder (or attach it as a Project knowledge file).
5. From now on, when you ask Claude to write a listing, it reads the Manifest first and writes in your voice.

The prompt

You are going to extract my personal writing voice from 5 listings I wrote.
Your output will be saved as a Claude Skill called "voice.md" so that every
future listing I write with you reads in MY voice, not in the generic
realtor average.

Read the 5 listings below carefully. Then produce a Voice Manifest with
exactly these sections, in this order:

1. SEVEN VOICE RULES
   The seven things that consistently characterize how I write. Be
   specific and observable. Example rule format:
   - "Opens with a sensory or spatial detail, never with the bedroom count"
   - "Sentences average 12-16 words; uses fragments for emphasis"
   - "Second person ('you') used sparingly, only for closing"
   - "Never uses 'stunning', 'must-see', 'gem', 'oasis', 'sanctuary'"

2. FIVE PHRASES I ACTUALLY USE
   Direct quotes or paraphrased patterns that appear across multiple
   listings. Show the exact wording.

3. FIVE PHRASES I NEVER USE
   Words and phrases conspicuously absent from my writing — especially
   the AI-tells: "stunning", "nestled", "boasts", "showcases", "tranquil",
   "must-see", "true gem", "well-appointed", "perfectly situated", etc.
   List the 5 most relevant ones I avoid.

4. MY CLOSING PATTERN
   How my listings end. Do I close with a CTA? An understatement? A
   neighborhood reference? Pull the pattern out as a one-line rule.

5. ONE-LINE VOICE SUMMARY
   A single sentence another writer could use to sound like me.
   Format: "Write like a realtor who [X], who [Y], and who never [Z]."

After the Manifest, give me a SAMPLE rewrite: take a generic 3-sentence
listing description (3 bedrooms, updated kitchen, fenced yard, good
neighborhood) and rewrite it in my voice, so I can see the Manifest
working before I save it.

Be honest. If two listings are noticeably different in voice, flag it
and ask me which one is more "me". Do not invent rules I don't follow.

After you save it

Every time you want a listing, tell Claude: "Write a listing for [address / property notes]. Use my voice from voice.md."

If you use Claude Code, drop voice.md into .claude/skills/voice/SKILL.md with a one-line frontmatter description, and Claude will auto-load it whenever the conversation touches listings.

If you're on Claude.ai (web), attach voice.md as a Project file. Same effect.

One honest caveat

The Manifest is only as good as the 5 listings you paste in. If you pick your weakest 5, you'll get an average voice. Pick the listings you'd be proud to show a peer. That's the voice worth saving.

The Weekend Off Stack

Three Claude Skills that replaced my Sunday open houses, my weekend buyer texts, and my Monday-morning scramble.

The promise: Run these three Skills consistently for one month and you stop working Sundays. Same clients. Same deals. More sleep.

How it works: Saturday Showing Brief preps every showing in 3 minutes Friday night. Always-On Listing Q&A answers buyer questions while you sleep. Monday Followup Stack pre-drafts your week-start emails by Friday 4pm so you skim-and-send instead of writing-from-scratch.

What you need: Claude (any plan) and 20 minutes to install all three. Save each as a Skill once and you never re-paste these prompts again.

01

Saturday Showing Brief

Run Friday 9pm

A one-page brief per property. So Saturday morning you walk in knowing more than the listing agent.

How it works

Friday night, paste tomorrow’s addresses + your buyer’s budget and timeline. Claude returns one printable brief per property: recent comps, last-sold price, days on market, three features that match THIS buyer, and the one question to ask to learn what they actually want.

You walk in prepared. The buyer thinks you spent hours.

The prompt

You are my Saturday showing prep assistant. I’m showing a buyer
properties tomorrow morning. Build me a one-page brief PER property
that I can print and read in the car.

For each address below, produce this exact structure:

ADDRESS LINE 1
ADDRESS LINE 2

THE QUICK READ (3 lines max)
- Beds / baths / sqft
- Listed price / days on market / price changes
- Last sold (year + price) so I know the seller’s position

THREE TALKING POINTS for THIS buyer
Tie each one to what the buyer told me they care about (see context below).
Specific, factual, not generic. Skip features that don’t match the buyer.

ONE OBJECTION TO PRE-EMPT
The most likely thing the buyer will say no to. Pre-frame it before they
bring it up.

ONE QUESTION TO ASK
After the tour. One open-ended question that surfaces whether this house
is actually the one, without sounding like a sales close.

Keep each brief under 200 words. Plain English. No real-estate cliches.

BUYER CONTEXT
- Budget: $___
- Timeline: ___ (e.g. "needs to be in by Sept", "no rush")
- Must-haves: ___ (e.g. "ground-floor primary", "fenced yard")
- Dealbreakers: ___ (e.g. "no HOA over $200/mo")
- Their words about what they want: ___ (paste a direct quote if you have one)

ADDRESSES TO PREP

1. ___
2. ___
3. ___
4. ___

Save it as a Skill

Drop into .claude/skills/saturday-brief/SKILL.md with a one-line frontmatter. Or on Claude.ai web, save as a Project file. Then on Friday night, just say “Use Saturday Brief for these 4 addresses,” and Claude pulls the rest.

02

Always-On Listing Q&A

Set once per listing

A page per listing that answers buyer questions in your voice 24/7. Cuts the Sunday-night text thread before it starts.

How it works

For every new listing, give Claude the property details + your usual buyer FAQ patterns. Claude generates a buyer-facing Q&A page (you host on a free link — Google Docs, Notion, your IDX site). Put the link in your listing description. Buyer questions get answered at midnight, in your voice, without waking you.

The same prompt produces a tab-able document the buyer can browse: "Schools", "HOA", "Yard", "Commute" — each section pre-answered.

The prompt

You are building an always-on buyer Q&A page for one of my listings.
It will live as a public link. Buyers browse it any hour, get answers in
my voice, and only contact me when they’re ready to schedule a showing.

Generate the page in plain HTML I can paste into Google Docs, Notion, or
my site. Use these sections only (skip any that don’t apply):

1. THE 30-SECOND SUMMARY
2. SCHOOLS & ZONING
3. HOA / CONDO / TAXES
4. RECENT WORK & INSPECTIONS
5. LAYOUT & SQUARE FOOTAGE
6. NEIGHBORHOOD & COMMUTE
7. SHOWING & OFFER PROCESS

Rules:
- Every answer is a direct sentence. No "stunning", "must-see", "perfect".
- If a question doesn’t have data, write "Ask agent — numbers vary".
  Never make up specifics.
- Fair Housing safe. Describe property + geography, not buyers.
- End with: "Ready to see it? Reply to the listing or text [my number]."

LISTING DETAILS
- Address: ___
- Listed price: ___
- Beds / baths / sqft / lot: ___
- Year built / last renovated: ___
- HOA / taxes / utilities: ___
- Recent work: ___
- School district: ___
- Anything unusual (easement, history, etc.): ___

MY VOICE (paste your Voice Manifest here if you have one, or list:
- 3 phrases you use
- 3 phrases you never use
- Your typical closing line
)

Where to put the page

Cheapest path: paste output into a public Google Doc, copy the link, drop the link into your listing description ("Full Q&A: [link]"). Buyers tour the document at 11pm. You get a notification only when they hit "Reply to the listing."

03

Monday Followup Stack

Auto-drafted by Sunday night

Every Saturday showing becomes a Monday-morning email by Sunday night. You skim and send in 12 minutes.

How it works

After each showing, paste two lines into Claude: address + your one-sentence read on what the buyer said. By Sunday night, Claude has a Monday email pre-drafted per buyer with the right follow-up move: send-more-listings, offer-strategy, or close-the-loop-and-stay-warm. You skim, edit one line, send.

The prompt

You are my Monday-morning followup writer. After Saturday showings, I
paste my read of each buyer below. Generate ONE email per buyer to send
Monday at 8am, in my voice.

The email must do all four:
1. Reference something specific from THIS Saturday’s tour (not generic)
2. Surface the buyer’s next decision point (more homes? offer? wait?)
3. Give them ONE action to take this week
4. Keep me visible without sounding desperate

Email length: 5-7 sentences. Subject line: 5 words max, no emojis.

For each buyer, classify the followup type and write accordingly:
- HOT: ready to offer. Email opens an offer conversation.
- WARM: liked one but not committed. Email proposes next 2 showings.
- COOL: nothing clicked. Email keeps me top-of-mind without nagging.
- COLD-LIST: not really buying yet. Bi-weekly market note instead.

Format your output as:

=== BUYER 1: [Name] ===
TYPE: HOT / WARM / COOL / COLD-LIST
SUBJECT: ___
BODY:
___

=== BUYER 2: ... ===
(same structure, repeat)

SATURDAY READS

Buyer 1: ___ [name, one-line your honest read]
Buyer 2: ___
Buyer 3: ___

The Sunday rhythm

Sunday 7pm: paste your reads (60 seconds). Sunday 7:02pm: emails are drafted. Skim, fix one line per email, schedule for Monday 8am. Total: 12 minutes. Sunday 7:15pm: you’re done.
